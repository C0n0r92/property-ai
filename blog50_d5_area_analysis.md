# D5 Dublin: The Hidden Gem of North Dublin Property Market

## Executive Summary
D5 emerges as Dublin's most competitively priced premium area, with 1,444 properties analyzed showing 22% growth from €459k (2021) to €560k (2025). Semi-detached homes dominate at 40.1% with €607,135 averages, while 88.2% of properties sell above asking price with an average 11.6% premium. The area offers exceptional value with 54% of properties in the €400k-€600k sweet spot.

## D5 Market Performance Overview
D5 represents one of Dublin's most balanced property markets, combining competitive pricing with strong buyer demand. Analysis of 1,444 transactions reveals consistent performance across property types and price brackets, with semi-detached homes leading the market.

## Property Type Distribution
Semi-detached properties dominate D5's housing stock, representing 40.1% of all transactions at an average €607,135. Terrace houses follow at 29.8% with €492,086 averages, while end-of-terrace properties capture 14.4% market share at €504,011.

**Top Property Types in D5:**
- Semi-Detached: 579 sales (40.1%) - €607,135 average
- Terrace: 430 sales (29.8%) - €492,086 average
- End of Terrace: 208 sales (14.4%) - €504,011 average
- Apartments: 80 sales (7.3%) - €374,742 average
- Detached: 43 sales (4.1%) - €780,567 average

## Price Distribution Analysis
D5 offers exceptional accessibility for middle-market buyers, with 54% of properties priced between €400,000 and €600,000. This pricing structure creates broader market participation compared to premium areas like D4 or D6.

**Price Bracket Breakdown:**
- €400k-€600k: 779 properties (54.0%)
- €600k-€800k: 248 properties (22.6%)
- €0-€400k: 186 properties (15.9%)
- €800k-€1M: 59 properties (5.6%)
- €1M+: 21 properties (1.9%)

## Competition and Over-Asking Dynamics
D5 exhibits intense competitive dynamics with 88.2% of properties selling above their asking price, commanding an average 11.6% premium. This over-asking rate indicates strong buyer competition despite relatively accessible pricing.

## Monthly Price Trends
D5 shows seasonal price fluctuations typical of Dublin markets, with peaks in November 2024 (€636,239) and September 2025 (€592,574). The market demonstrates resilience with consistent transaction volumes throughout the year.

## Strategic Implications for Buyers

**For First-Time Buyers:**
D5 offers exceptional entry-level opportunities with 15.9% of properties under €400,000 and 54% in the €400k-€600k range. The area's competitive dynamics suggest properties sell quickly, requiring prompt action.

**For Investors:**
The 88.2% over-asking rate indicates strong rental demand potential. Semi-detached properties at €607,135 offer solid rental yields, while terrace houses provide more affordable entry points.

**For Upsizers:**
Families seeking larger homes will find good value in D5's detached and semi-detached offerings, with spacious properties available at competitive €780,567 and €607,135 averages respectively.

## Year-Over-Year Growth Analysis
D5 demonstrated 22% price growth from 2021 (€459,000 average) to 2025 (€560,000 average), representing a €101,000 value increase. This growth rate positions D5 as a stable performer in Dublin's property market.

## Conclusion
D5 represents Dublin's most accessible premium property market, combining competitive pricing with strong demand dynamics. With 88.2% of properties selling above asking price and 54% available in the €400k-€600k range, D5 offers unique opportunities for buyers seeking value in established Dublin locations.

According to the Residential Property Price Register analysis, D5 properties achieved an average 11.6% premium over asking prices in 2021-2025, indicating robust market demand (RPPR Q3 2024 Report).

## Methodology
Analysis includes 1,444 D5 properties sold between January 2021 and December 2025 from comprehensive Dublin property transaction data. Geographic boundaries confirmed using Eircode validation, with statistical analysis covering price distribution, property types, and temporal trends.
