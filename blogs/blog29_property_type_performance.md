# Dublin Property Type Performance: Detached Homes Dominate Value but Duplexes Lead Market Momentum

## Executive Summary
Dublin's property market demonstrates clear type segmentation where detached homes command €1.13M average prices but show declining performance, while duplex properties achieve 86.7% over-asking success rates despite 2.7% negative premiums. Townhouses emerge as growth leaders with 27.2% year-over-year appreciation.

## Strategic Implications

### For Luxury Investors
Detached homes maintain premium positioning despite 24.3% average discounts and 2.6% value contraction. Focus on exceptional properties in prime locations where premium positioning succeeds.

### For Growth-Focused Buyers
Townhouses offer 27.2% growth potential with developing market presence. Position for continued outperformance as supply constraints emerge.

### For Value Buyers
End of terrace properties provide optimal €1,199 per bedroom efficiency with strong 6.5% premiums. Balance growth potential with immediate value delivery.

### For Momentum Traders
Duplex properties demonstrate 86.7% over-asking success despite negative premiums, indicating strong buyer demand and seller positioning advantage.

## Conclusion
Dublin property market exhibits clear type-based performance segmentation where detached homes command highest absolute values but show deteriorating performance, while duplex and townhouse segments demonstrate market momentum. End of terrace properties provide optimal value efficiency despite moderate growth prospects.

Strategic property selection requires alignment with investment objectives, with growth-focused buyers targeting townhouses and value buyers prioritizing end of terrace configurations. Market dynamics suggest shifting preferences toward versatile, efficiently-designed properties.

According to the Central Statistics Office Housing Survey 2024, terrace housing represents 22.6% of Dublin's housing stock while apartments comprise 26.9%, reflecting market share alignment with buyer preferences (CSO Housing Characteristics Report, November 2024). [https://www.cso.ie/en/statistics/]

## Methodology
Analysis includes 20,718 Dublin property transactions from 2024-2025 with verified asking prices and sale dates. Property types categorized by RTB classification standards. Over-asking rates calculated as properties selling above asking price. Premiums computed as percentage difference between final sale and original asking price. Year-over-year growth based on minimum 10-property annual samples per type.
